# Web Clippers Save Pages. ImageSnap Saves Structured Data.

You've probably tried saving product research with a web clipper — Notion Web Clipper, Pinterest, Eagle, or even just browser bookmarks.

It works at first. You clip a few pages, pin a few images. But a month later, you have 200 saved items and no idea which product was $15 vs $150, which supplier shipped fast, or why you saved half of them in the first place.

The problem isn't saving. It's saving *without context*.

---

## The Clipper Gap

Web clippers are designed to save content. They do that well. What they don't do is capture structured, queryable data.

**What you get with a typical web clipper:**

| Tool | What it saves | What it misses |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Notion Web Clipper | Full page HTML/markdown | No structured fields, no image organization |
| Pinterest | Image + link | No price, no description, no custom data |
| Eagle | Image + tags | No spreadsheet integration, no bulk data view |
| Browser bookmarks | URL only | Everything else |

The result is always the same: a collection of "stuff" with no structure. You can't sort by price, filter by supplier, or compare products across a table view — because the data was never captured that way.

## What ImageSnap Does Differently

ImageSnap doesn't save pages. It captures **images + designed context** into tools you already use.

**When you click the extension:**

1. Product images → saved to your Google Drive (organized, not dumped)
2. Page data → auto-filled into your Google Sheet (title, price, description, source URL)
3. Your custom fields → you define what matters (project name, rating, supplier, notes, status)

The result is a **searchable, sortable spreadsheet** with linked images — not a folder of screenshots you'll never look at again.

## FAQ

### Can I use ImageSnap alongside Notion?

Yes. Many users keep Notion for notes and general knowledge, and use ImageSnap specifically for product research that needs structured data and image organization.

### Does ImageSnap replace Pinterest for mood boards?

Not exactly. Pinterest is great for visual browsing and inspiration. ImageSnap is for research where you need to track specific details — prices, suppliers, specs — alongside images. Different tools for different jobs.

### Can I add my own fields?

Yes. You define your own context fields — project name, rating, supplier, status, notes, or anything else. ImageSnap auto-fills what it can from the page, and you fill in the rest.

### Where do my images go?

To your own Google Drive, in organized folders. Not to a third-party server, not embedded in a page — your Drive, your files.

### Is there a limit on how much I can save?

The free tier includes 30 captures per month. Paid plans offer more. Your Google Sheets and Drive storage limits are separate and depend on your Google account.
