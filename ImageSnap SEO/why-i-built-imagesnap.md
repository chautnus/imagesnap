I didn't set out to build a product. I set out to fix a workflow that was wasting my time.

[**Your origin story here:** What were you doing when you realized the problem? Were you researching products for a project? Tracking competitors? Building a catalog? What was the specific moment of frustration?]

The workflow was always the same: find a product page, copy the title, paste it into a spreadsheet, save the image, upload it to Drive, paste the link, add my notes. Repeat. For hours.

It wasn't hard. It was just slow, repetitive, and error-prone. And I couldn't believe there wasn't a tool that solved it.

## The Tools I Tried First

[**Your real experience here.** Which tools did you actually try? What was wrong with each one? Be specific — readers trust specificity.]

I tried the obvious options:

- **Web clippers** saved the page but not structured data. I had a Notion full of clipped pages and no way to compare products side by side.
- **Scrapers** were overkill. I didn't need 10,000 records. I needed 50 good ones with images.
- **Pinterest** was great for visual inspiration but terrible for research. No prices, no specs, no custom fields.
- **Manual spreadsheets** worked but killed my productivity. Two hours of data entry for 50 products.

None of these solved the actual problem: saving an image *with its context* in a way that stayed useful.

---

## The "I'll Just Build It" Moment

[**Your real decision point.** What pushed you to start building? Was it a weekend project? A late-night frustration? Did someone say something that made it click?]

At some point I realized what I actually wanted:

1. Click a button on any product page.
2. Images go to my Google Drive.
3. Product data goes to my Google Sheet.
4. I add my own fields — notes, ratings, project tags.
5. Done. One click. Everything organized.

That's it. Not a new app to learn. Not a database to manage. Just my existing Drive and Sheets, populated automatically.

## What ImageSnap Actually Is

I want to be clear about what ImageSnap does and doesn't do, because I've seen too many tools overpromise.

**ImageSnap saves any image with your designed context.**

Images go to your Google Drive. Context goes to your Google Sheet. They stay tied to one record. That's the product.

It's not an AI training platform. It's not a web scraper. It's not an analytics dashboard. It's a capture tool that respects your workflow and your data ownership.

You browse. You see something worth saving. You click. The image and its context are organized and yours.

## What I've Learned So Far

[**Your real learnings.** These make the story credible. Some examples you might adapt:]

- Building for yourself is clarifying but dangerous — you have to test with people who aren't you.
- "Simple" products are hard to explain. People assume there must be more to it.
- The hardest positioning question: is this a tool or a platform? (Answer: launch as a tool, think like a platform.)
- Data ownership isn't a feature — it's a trust signal. People care where their images go.
