# The Swipe File Tool Marketers Have Been Waiting For

Every marketer has a swipe file. The problem is where it lives.

Maybe it's a Pinterest board with 400 pins and no context. Maybe it's a folder of screenshots named `IMG_4392.png`. Maybe it's bookmarks you saved six months ago that now 404.

You saved it because it inspired you. But when you need it — for a campaign brief, a design review, a pitch deck — you can't find it. Or you find it but can't remember why you saved it, what brand it was, or what made it good.

A swipe file without context is just digital hoarding.

---

## What a Useful Swipe File Actually Needs

A swipe file that works has three things:

1. **The visual** — the ad, the landing page, the product shot, the email design
2. **The context** — brand name, date, channel, why it caught your eye, what technique it uses
3. **The structure** — a way to search, filter, and compare entries

Pinterest gives you #1. Notion gives you #2 (if you're disciplined). Nobody gives you all three without manual assembly.

## How Marketers Use ImageSnap as a Swipe File

ImageSnap captures images to your Google Drive and structured context to your Google Sheet — in one click. For marketers building a swipe file, this means:

### Set Up Your Swipe File Fields

Define the context that makes each entry useful:

- **Brand** — who made this?
- **Channel** — where did you see it? (ad, email, landing page, social)
- **Category** — what type of content is this? (hero image, product shot, ad creative, email header)
- **Why saved** — what caught your eye?
- **Technique** — what marketing technique does it demonstrate? (social proof, urgency, lifestyle, UGC)
- **Date captured** — when did you see it?
- **Rating** — how good is it? (1–5)

### Capture While You Browse

See a great ad? Click the extension. Browsing a competitor's landing page? Click. Found an email design worth remembering? Screenshot it, then capture with context.

Each capture takes 10–15 seconds. The image goes to Drive. The context goes to Sheets.

### Use It When You Need It

Two months later, you're working on a new campaign. You open your Sheet and:

- Filter by `Channel: email` to see email design inspiration
- Sort by `Rating: 5` to see your best finds
- Search for `Technique: social proof` to find proof-point examples
- Filter by `Brand: [competitor]` to see their creative evolution

Everything is there. Searchable. Sortable. With images linked and ready to reference.

## FAQ

### Can I capture ads from social media?

Yes. If you can see it in your browser, you can capture it. Browse Facebook ads, Instagram in browser, or Twitter — and click to capture. Images save to Drive, context to Sheets.

### Can my whole marketing team use the same swipe file?

Yes. Share the Google Sheet and Drive folder with your team. Everyone captures into the same database. Filter by team member if you want to see who found what.

### What if I want to capture a full landing page, not just product images?

You can screenshot the page and capture that screenshot with context. ImageSnap is optimized for product images, but any image in your browser can be captured with your custom fields attached.

### How is this different from just using a Google Sheet manually?

The capture step. Without ImageSnap, you'd need to: screenshot → save to Drive → copy the link → paste in Sheet → type the metadata. With ImageSnap, you click once and add your notes. The image handling is automatic.
